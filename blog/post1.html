Internet History Follow Up

E-commerce

July 3rd, 2023

E-commerce started due to the benefit of the invention of the Electronic Data Interchange during the 1960s. Being able to transfer data and information via the internet allowed the possibility of making transactions online. The idea of online shopping was first introduced in the 80s in the United Kingdom by Michael Aldrich, who made transactions between business and business come true. Public consumers weren’t in the E-commerce market until the 1990s when the World Wide Web was developed by Tim Berners-Lee. It was a significant leap forward in the history of the Internet that gave the public general access to the Internet where people can purchase and sell products.

The invention of the internet brought a lot of possibilities in E-commerce. Numerous companies and businesses arose in the 90s trying to have a share of the pie. The Dotcom bubble formed as a result of low-interest rates and everyone’s interest in investing in new companies; however, the bubble burst in 2000 for several reasons: high-interest rates, recession, and overinflation in the market. The E-commerce market grew rapidly in the 90s, but many of the then-famous businesses declined and disappeared after the Dotcom bust, such as Pets.com and Boo.com. Amazon and eBay are still leading forces in the E-commerce market today.

Online shopping has benefited humans for decades now, and it is undoubtedly providing more convenient lifestyles for humans as well as platforms for businesses to expand their market and audiences. I shop on the Internet regularly, and in fact a lot of the time online shopping is my first option. Especially during the pandemic, I benefited a lot from E-commerce because of how convenient it is.
