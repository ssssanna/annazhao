Lower Antelope Canyon

About

The Antelope Canyon is a natural photogenic site located in Page, Arizona. Lower and upper Antelope Canyon exhibit different beauty while they are not far away from each other. The canyon is composed of sandstone, and under the influence of rain, floods and wind, the walls of stones demonstrate a flowing form that is unique and spectacular. With the change of light and shadow during the day, inside the canyon also show various colors and scenery that attract tourists from all over the world. The Antelope Canyon is owned by the Navajo Nation, and it is of great significance to the Navajo tribe.

Travel Routes

The closest airport to Lower Antelope Canyon is Page Municipal Airport in Page, Arizona. Most tourists may also choose to land in Las Vegas or Phoenix International Airport (PHX) and then drive to Page, Arizona.

We drove from Bryce Canyon National Park to Arizona, and we spent almost 3 hours on the road. There are a lot of other famous scenery sites in Page, such as Lake Powell, the Horseshoe Bend, the Glen Canyon Dam, and so on. Each scenic spot is not far from each other if driving.

Travel Plog

When we arrived in Arizona, we visited Lake Powell first and then we attended a tour at Lower Antelope Canyon. The Antelope Canyon is open for public access but tourists are not allowed to enter the canyon without a local tour guide. There are lots of tour guide groups available for booking online, and it is important to compare and contrast to pick one that works best for you. We waited in line for a couple of hours, and went down a few flights of stairs to finally go into the canyon where we were stunned by the natural wonder in front of us.

The sandstones are carved by water and wind, and each exhibits a different pattern. We went down into the canyon around 4 in the afternoon when the sunlight shined right onto the sandstones. Colors were vibrant while very eye-catching. Each line on the stones flows smoothly and tells its own history through its textures and patterns.
